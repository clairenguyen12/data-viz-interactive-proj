Exploring gentrification trends of Chicago neighborhoods

Gentrification refers to the process of altering the demographic composition of a neighborhood. In Chicago, this process usually includes the replacement of low-income and/or minority residents by white, higher-income residents. The process typically lasts over several years. Gentrification has been a debatable policy issue because displaced residents believe that the influx of new residents prices out old residents and destabilizes their communities. On the other hand, gentrification also leads to lower crime rates and economic improvement in a neighborhood. Areas in Chicago that have been experiencing gentrification include Logan Square, Pilsen, Uptown, etc.
